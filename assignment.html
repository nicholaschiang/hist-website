Research Question

Researching the Internet on the Internet

To what extent has the development of the Internet changed how we communicate, find, and distribute information and how has this change occurred?

Thesis

How we came to use the Internet everyday

The Internet has completely changed the way that we communicate and acquire knowledge. We now expect information and communication to be instantaneous: expectations that have been met and surpassed with consistency by modern protocol and technology standards.

This change occured over the span of about 40 years, from the first government ARPANET to the plethora of inter-connected ISP networks that exist today. Innovation, however, does not have a timeline for anyone and is constantly evolving. Even today, we are in the middle of a world-wide transistion from an older web protocol called IPv4 to a newer protocol (IPv6) that accomodates the unprecedented growth in internet-enabled devices.

Topic and Justification

A brief history of the Internet

We use the Internet everyday. Whether we realize it or not, the World Wide Web is just so engrained in our modern society that it is impossible to go a day (at least living in Palo Alto) without feeling its presence. I examine the origins of this incredible tool and its evolution through time. I will argue for net neutrality and work to inform those who visit the site on the importance of such policies.

This topic belongs in a world history class for many reasons: most of which are so obvious that stating them would be unnecessary, but I'll give you a few. First, the Internet touches almost every aspect of the modern world. The fact that information travels as fast as it does it due to the Internet and thus it follows that it is the Internet that enables reporting and news/history to be so wide spread and easily accessible. Second, the Internet is the latest major development in the history of mankind. I can think of no other invention in the last century that has had more profound an impact than the World Wide Web.
